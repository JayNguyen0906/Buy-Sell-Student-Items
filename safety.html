Safety Tips

Your safety is our top priority. Follow these guidelines to ensure safe transactions.

While most transactions on our platform go smoothly, it's important to take precautions when buying and selling items. We've compiled these safety tips to help you have a positive experience.

Account Safety

Protecting your account is the first step to a safe experience on our platform.

  • Use a strong, unique password - Create a password that's at least 12 characters long with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Never share your password - Our staff will never ask for your password. If someone claims to be from our team and asks for your password, report them immediately.
  • Use your university email - Always register with your university email address to help us maintain a trusted community.
  • Enable two-factor authentication - Add an extra layer of security to your account by enabling 2FA in your account settings.
  • Log out on shared computers - Always log out when using public or shared computers to prevent unauthorized access to your account.

Pro Tip: Regularly check your account activity for any suspicious actions you don't recognize. If you notice anything unusual, change your password immediately and contact our support team.

Meeting Safety

When it's time to meet and complete your transaction, follow these guidelines to stay safe:

  • Meet in public places - Always meet in busy, well-lit public areas on campus. Good options include the student union, library, or campus coffee shops.
  • Meet during daylight hours - Try to schedule meetings during daytime when more people are around.
  • Bring a friend - If possible, don't go alone, especially for high-value items or when meeting someone for the first time.
  • Tell someone where you're going - Let a friend or family member know where you're meeting, who you're meeting, and when you expect to return.
  • Use designated safe exchange zones - Many campuses have designated areas for exchanges that may be monitored by security cameras.
  • Trust your instincts - If something feels off about the meeting or the person, don't hesitate to cancel or reschedule.

Warning: Never meet at private residences, isolated locations, or in vehicles for first-time meetings. Don't invite strangers to your dorm or apartment, and don't go to theirs.

Recommended Meeting Spots on Campus:

  • Student Union Building lobby
  • Main Library entrance
  • Campus cafeterias during busy hours
  • University Center
  • Near campus security office

Payment Safety

Protect yourself from payment scams with these guidelines:

  • Use cash for in-person transactions - Cash is the safest method for in-person exchanges.
  • Count money in person - Always count cash at the time of the transaction, before handing over the item.
  • Be wary of payment apps - If using payment apps (like Venmo or Cash App), confirm the payment has been completed and is in your account before handing over items.
  • Inspect items before paying - Thoroughly check that the item matches the description and is in the expected condition before completing payment.
  • Keep receipts - For higher-value items, consider writing a simple receipt that both parties sign.

Red Flags - Avoid These Payment Methods:

  • Wire transfers
  • Money orders or cashier's checks
  • Payment requests from unknown apps
  • Requests to ship items before payment clears
  • Overpayment scenarios where the seller asks you to return the difference

Communication Safety

Safe communication practices help protect your privacy and prevent scams:

  • Keep communication on our platform - Use our messaging system for all transaction-related communication so we have a record if issues arise.
  • Protect personal information - Don't share unnecessary personal details like your home address, financial information, or ID numbers.
  • Be wary of urgent requests - Scammers often create false urgency to pressure you into making quick decisions.
  • Watch for red flags in messages - Poor grammar, requests to use alternate payment methods, or offers that seem too good to be true are warning signs.
  • Document your conversations - Keep records of your messages in case you need to report an issue later.

Example of a Safe Initial Message:
"Hi, I'm interested in the textbook you listed. Is it still available? I'm on campus Monday-Thursday and could meet at the Student Union if that works for you. Thanks!"
